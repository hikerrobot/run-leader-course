# LIRF

## Role of the run leader

- Organise and promote the group/club
- Provide a safe and inclusive environment
- Encourage and maintain involvement in running and walking
- Direct runners to development opportunities
- Ensure good sportmanship
- They need to be individual centred leaders

## Factors which may affect interest in running

- Psychological
- Culture
- Social
- Economic
- Impairment

## Why people become run leaders

- Share running enjoyment with others
- Achieve personal ambition
- Meet people
- Encourage people to athletics
- Earn money

## Individual centered

- Put needs of individuals over the interest of themselves or the group
- Encourage individuals to be involved in their own development
- Empower individuals to take responsibility for their development
- Ensure all individuals are welcomed and encouraged to explore their potential
- Recognise the importance of providing a fun and safe environment

### Achieving individual centered leading

- Discover preferred communication style
- Welcome each and every runner
- Find out needs, motives and interests
- Adapt your communication style
- Seek advice when necessary
- Buddy up new runners, looking for volunteers
- Asking if runner has fears, questions, motivations, previous experience

### Disabilities

Particularly important to ensure runners with disabilities have the opportunity to participate.
