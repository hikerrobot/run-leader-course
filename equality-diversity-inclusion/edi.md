# Equality, diversion and inclusion

## Equality

The term _equality_ means that everyone should be treated fairly.

## Diversity

This term has many definitions.

A common theme however, is that they all recognise that an individuals background, knowledge, skills, aptitudes and experiences all differ.

These differences should be valued and appreciated.

# Inclusion

Inclusion in sport means ensuring everyone can participate, removing any barriers and treating people with respect.

## Inequalities

A run leader must treat all people equally.
You must not judge people until you know them.


## Discrimination

You must not treat some any more or less favourably, based on any of the things which make them who they are. For example, their sex, race, age. Anything which makes them who they are essentially.

Types:
- Direct discrimation
- Indirect discrimination
- Victimisation
- Harassment
- Reverse

### Positive action

Positive action to combat discrimination is ok. Reverse discrimination is not.
Positive action is seeking addressing an imbalance in a entity or where a group is disadvantaged amongst existing members.

### Indirect

Where something is enforced and applies to everyone but disdvantages people who share a protected characteristic.

### Victimisation

Occurs when you complain about discrimination or support someone who is a victim and are mistreated or suffer as a result.

### Harassment

Being mistreated in relation to a particular protected characteristic

### Reverse

A scenario where a previously majority group is now discriminated against in favour of a group that is a minority group.

## Unconcious bias

An example of this is choosing to work with someone who is the same age as you. Or someone you have something in common with, even if they are not the most suitable person.

## Stereotypes

Although positive strerotypes exist, it is thought that all sterotypes are damaging.

A stereotype could be choosing someone stocky to do building work because you assume their strength to be an advantage.
