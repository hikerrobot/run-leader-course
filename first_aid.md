# First Aid

## First Aid principes

- If it's hot, cool it
- If it's broken, don't move it
- If it's bleeding, stop it

## Common incidents

- Nose bleeds
- Fainting
- Insect bites and stings
- Electrocution/electrical burn
- Environmental iussues
- Soft tissue injuries
- Low blood sugar
- Asthma

## Nose bleed

If someone has a nose bleed, they should tilt their head forwards.
Phone for an ambulance is the person is taking anti-coagulants.

## Fainting

Fainting occurs when blood flow to the brain is reduced.
If someone faints, lie them down on the floor. This ensures the blood can get to vital organs.

## Insect bites and stings

### Bites

With bites and stings, they can be treated using the _if it's hot, cool it_ treatment.

Symptoms should subside in a few hours.

### Stings

Can be painful and sore for a few days.
Treat like bites with cold cloths.

The exception is bee stings. The sting needs to be scraped out, using something with a hard edge. Then treat as a bite, with cool cloths or ice.

## Electrocution

Always ensure the power is off, before helping.

Low voltage shock can result in small burns.

If someone has been burnt, they need to seek further medical advice. There may be internal tissue damage.

## Environmental issues

- Sunburn
- Heatstroke
- Hypothermia
- Dehyrdation

### Heatstroke

Signs are confusion and hyperventalating.

Individual should be removed from heat, laid down and cooled. Or given cold water.

### Hypothermia

Signs:
- Shivering
- Feeling cold
- Low energy
- Cold, pale skin

To deal with this, they need to warm up. 
Warm drinks and high energy food help.

### Dehydration

- Avoid alcohol and caffeine
- Move them to shade
- Need to stop physical activity
- Hydrate

## Soft tissue injuries

Both are tears.

- Sprain. Torn ligament.
- Strain. Torn muscle or tendon.

Strains are usually occur from sports.

RICE is the best treatment:

- Rest
- Ice
- Compress
- Elevate

## Low blood sugar

Sign and symptoms:

- Rapid change in behaviour
- Pale, cold and sweaty skin
- Shallow rapid breaths
- Strange behaviour

Do not given the individual food or drink if their conciousness deteriorates.

## Asthma

Narrows the airway, causing breathing difficulties.
Cold air can makes the symptoms worse.

If extreme symptoms, their lips or nails may turn blue.

If the symptoms get worse even when using a reliever, phone for an ambulance.

Things to do:

- Help them sit upright and control their breathing
- Only lay them down if they become unconcious
